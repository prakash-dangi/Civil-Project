Theory of Elastic Moduli

Elastic moduli are fundamental material properties that describe the response of a material to deformation under applied forces. There are three main elastic moduli:

Elastic Modulus (E)

Elastic Modulus (also known as Young's Modulus) is a measure of a material's stiffness. It quantifies how much a material will deform under a given load. The formula is given by:

\[ E = 2G(1 + \mu) \]

where \( E \) is the Elastic Modulus, \( G \) is the Rigidity Modulus, and \( \mu \) is Poisson's Ratio.

Rigidity Modulus (G)

Rigidity Modulus measures the material's resistance to shearing deformation. It is related to the shear stress and shear strain in a material. The formula is given by:

\[ G = \frac{E}{2(1 + \mu)} \]

where \( G \) is the Rigidity Modulus, \( E \) is the Elastic Modulus, and \( \mu \) is Poisson's Ratio.

Bulk Modulus (K)

Bulk Modulus measures the material's resistance to uniform compression or expansion. It is related to the hydrostatic stress and volumetric strain in a material. The formula is given by:

\[ K = \frac{E}{3(1 - 2\mu)} \]

where \( K \) is the Bulk Modulus, \( E \) is the Elastic Modulus, and \( \mu \) is Poisson's Ratio.

Poisson's Ratio (\(\mu\))

Poisson's Ratio measures the deformation in the direction perpendicular to the applied force. It is the ratio of transverse contraction strain to longitudinal extension strain. The formula is given by:

\[ \mu = \frac{E}{2G} - 1 \]

where \( \mu \) is Poisson's Ratio, \( E \) is the Elastic Modulus, and \( G \) is the Rigidity Modulus.
